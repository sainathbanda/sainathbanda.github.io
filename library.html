💡 How to Access Paywalled Papers Legally (as an ICAR Scientist)

METHOD 01

ICAR-NAARM e-Library

ICAR scientists get free access to thousands of journals via ICAR's institutional subscriptions through NAARM library. Email your institute librarian for login credentials.

METHOD 02

Email the Author

Find the corresponding author's email (on the paper or ResearchGate) and email them directly. Authors are legally allowed to share their own papers — most respond within 48 hours.

METHOD 03

Unpaywall Browser Extension

Install the free Unpaywall extension in Chrome. It automatically finds legal free versions of papers you're viewing. Works on 50%+ of all papers.

METHOD 04

ResearchGate / Academia.edu

Many authors upload their papers to ResearchGate or Academia.edu. Search the exact paper title — often a free PDF is available from the authors themselves.

METHOD 05

Preprint Servers

Check bioRxiv, SSRN, EconStor, or AgEcon Search for preprint (pre-publication) versions of papers. Usually the same content, freely available.

METHOD 06

National Digital Library (NDL)

India's NDL (ndl.gov.in) provides free access to millions of resources including e-books, theses, and journal articles for Indian researchers and students.

Free Chrome/Firefox extension that automatically finds legal free versions of paywalled papers. A green tab appears when a free version exists. Works on 50%+ of all papers. Install this immediately — it will save you hundreds of hours.

Free reference manager with browser extension. Save papers with one click, auto-generate citations, organize your library, and sync across devices. The standard tool for thesis writing and literature management.

Paste any paper and see a visual map of related papers. Instantly find seminal works, recent follow-ups, and related literature. Free for 5 graphs/month. Invaluable for building your literature review.
