Sample Size Calculator

Calculate the required sample size for surveys, field experiments, and RCTs. Based on standard statistical formulas used in agricultural economics research.

Population Size (N)
Total farmers / households in your study area. Use 999999 if unknown.
Confidence Level
Standard: 95% (z=1.96). Use 99% for high-stakes policy research.
Margin of Error (e)
Acceptable error in your estimate (e.g. 0.05 = ±5%).
Expected Proportion (p)
Expected proportion with the characteristic. Use 0.5 for maximum sample size.
Non-response / Attrition (%)
Add buffer for non-responses. Typical: 10–20%.
Design Effect (DEFF)
For cluster sampling, multiply by DEFF (typically 1.5–2.0). Use 1 for SRS.
Mean of Control Group (μ₁)
Expected mean outcome in control group.
Expected Treatment Effect (Δ)
Minimum detectable effect (MDE) — smallest meaningful difference.
Standard Deviation (σ)
Expected SD of outcome variable (from pilot or literature).
Significance Level (α)
Probability of Type I error.
Power (1-β)
Probability of detecting a true effect. Standard: 80%.
Number of Arms
2 = treatment + control. More arms = larger total sample.
Proportion Group 1 (p₁)
Expected proportion in control group (e.g. adoption rate).
Proportion Group 2 (p₂)
Expected proportion in treatment/comparison group.
